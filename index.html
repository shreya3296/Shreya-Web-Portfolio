About Me

Hi! I am a second-year Master's student at Harvard University, where I focus on quantitative economics and data science. I have an undergraduate degree from BITS Pilani, India, where I majored in Computer Science with a minor in Finance.

Previously, I have worked in various roles at the intersection of technology and policy. Most recently, I was a data research associate for a suite of projects that used social networks and information theory to develop policy-relevant insights.

I am interested in working on interdisciplinary problems at the intersection of machine learning and economics. I am particularly interested in using Machine Learning models, with a specific focus on Bayesian models and representation learning (Recommender systems, Deep Learning). I am currently in search of a data science role that is both intellectually and professionally rewarding.

I was born in Varanasi, India, and attended nine schools before finally settling in Mumbai. I am currently working on a passion project that combines my love for traveling, reading, and writing into creating a series of articles on one book from each country (inspired by this blog.).
